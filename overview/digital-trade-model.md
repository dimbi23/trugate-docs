# Digital trade model

## The old model

Historically, trade, tax, customs, and finance operated as separate silos.

A business would:
1. issue an invoice,
2. later handle tax declarations,
3. separately prepare customs documentation,
4. then try to obtain payment or financing.

Each step required different documents, different data entry, and different validation processes.

## The new model

In the digital trade environment, those silos are converging into a continuous data flow.

The modern transaction lifecycle looks like this:
1. a commercial transaction is recorded,
2. the transaction is fiscally validated,
3. the transaction becomes usable for customs and cross-border workflows,
4. the trusted record can support finance and payments.

## Trugate’s place in the model

Trugate acts at the point where commercial activity becomes trusted digital evidence.

It captures the commercial transaction, validates it through compliance processes, stores it as a trusted record, and prepares it for downstream systems.

This is the core of the Trugate platform.
