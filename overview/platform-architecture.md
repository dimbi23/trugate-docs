# Platform architecture

Trugate is organized into four architectural layers.

## Layer 1 — Transaction capture

This layer records commercial activity in structured digital form.

Examples:
- invoices,
- counterparties,
- line items,
- transaction value,
- tax treatment.

## Layer 2 — Trust and compliance

This layer validates transactions through regulatory and policy rules.

Examples:
- NIF/STAT verification,
- invoice validation,
- fiscal clearance,
- audit trail,
- document authenticity.

## Layer 3 — Interoperability

This layer prepares trusted records for use by other systems.

Examples:
- customs-ready data,
- export metadata,
- standards mapping,
- digital credentials.

## Layer 4 — Activation

This layer enables value-added services based on trusted transaction data.

Examples:
- trade finance,
- payment triggers,
- trust scoring,
- readiness indicators.

## Design principle

Trugate begins with mandatory compliance, then expands into higher-value services.
