# Golden Triangle

Trugate is built around the convergence of three key domains:

| Pillar | Role | Key Data Object |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Trade | Commercial activity | Invoice |
| Fiscality | Legal validation | Clearance stamp |
| Customs | Border enforcement | Release / origin evidence |

## Why this matters

A transaction starts as an economic event. It becomes trustworthy only when institutions can validate it.

- **Trade** defines what happened.
- **Fiscality** validates that it is legal and correctly declared.
- **Customs** verifies movement across borders when relevant.

Trugate sits between these pillars and makes the transaction portable across them.

## The practical meaning of this model

When a company issues an invoice through Trugate:
- the trade event is captured,
- fiscal validation is performed through clearance,
- the resulting record becomes usable for future customs and finance workflows.

This is why Trugate is more than an invoicing interface. It is a trust layer.
